3.2. Bidirectional Setup

The following diagram illustrates a setup for a bilateral remote audio transmission. In this type of connection each location setup is configured to send its audio signal over the Internet to be output in the other location. For this you need identical audio setup in both locations.

Room 1 audio mixer setup

Room 2 audio mixer setup

Microphone in Mic IN (or other source) of mixer line 1.

Open Mic AUX (or line AUX) so you can send the microphone signal to the sound card and then to Room 2.

Turn OFF the Mic output level to main mix.

Microphone in Mic IN (or other source) of mixer line 1.

Open Mic AUX (or line AUX) so you can send the microphone signal to the sound card and then to Room 1.

Turn OFF the Mic output level to main mix.

Room 1 audio sent through AUX send 1&2 to sound card input 1&2 then to Room 2.

Room 2 audio sent through AUX send 1&2 to sound card input 1&2 then to Room 1.

Room 2 audio redirected through Room 1 sound card outputs 1&2 into mixer input lines 7&8.

Turn OFF AUX level of lines 7&8 to avoid resending signal to card.

Room 1 audio redirected through Room 2 sound card outputs 1&2 into mixer input lines 7&8.

Turn OFF AUX level of lines 7&8 to avoid resending signal to card.

Open main mix levels for lines 7&8 to hear Room 2 audio ouput on main mix.

Open main mix level for lines 7&8 to hear Room 1 audio ouput on main mix.
