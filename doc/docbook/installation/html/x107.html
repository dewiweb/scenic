2.2. Video Hardware Specifications

2.2.1. Graphic Card

The graphic card will manage your video output. The choice of your graphic card will depend on how you want to use Scenic. For now, we only support nvidia video card. Some ATI cards have problems with Xvideo extension. Before choosing your graphic card, determine on wich linux distribution and releases you are. Some graphic card may work only on certain releases. Before buying a graphic card, verify on the nvidia unix page that the hardware you are planning to use is supported not only by your linux distribution but also on the release you are using.

We need to define the type of video output you will need. Usually this output is sent to a video mixer, a video projector, a monitor or any video display. On the table below we list the various possible ouput on a graphic card.

2.2.2. Video Capture

The video capture is usally a card or a device connected on one end to your camera and on the other end, to your computer. Choosing your video capture device depends on the many factors:

  • The ouput cable on your camera

  • The output resolution of your camera

  • The video capture quality you need

  • The computer specification descriped in the previous section

  • The final output you need

Giving these factors, we suggest some devices ordered by the quality and performance that they provide.
