5.2. Video Settings

Here we will configure all the settings related to the video input and output

5.2.1. Video Output

First thing, make sure you have the Xvideo extension actived in the Xorg server: 

xvinfo | grep -i adaptor

The output should NOT be "no adaptors present". If it's not activated, you may have to use a proprietary driver. See NvidiaSettings or AtiConfig for Nvidia and ATI cards respectively.

Then, what we want now is two video output. One for your monitor screen and one other for your projector or video mixer.

For the nvidia graphic card, we have to detect both output. The first output (your monitor) is usually set to a 1024x768 resolution and the second output (VGA, or S-video Output) is usually set to a resolution of 800x600. Note that those resolution can vary from one setup to an other.

For this, follow our NvidiaSettings instructions.

Set the local machine display to output the video from the remote machine on the wanted monitor or TV.

The environment variable DISPLAY tells all X clients where to display their windows.

Ex: export DISPLAY=:0.0 will display the video on your local default screen. export DISPLAY=:0.1 will display the video on your second screen. 

export DISPLAY=:0.0

5.2.2. Video Input

Scenic can capture video from a video capture card through v4l2, from IIDC camera or DV camera (also called iLink). DV cameras and decks have a high latency (often between 150 and 250ms) and should not be the video input of choise for low latency requirements. =====5.1.2.1 Video4Linux ==== Plug in the video camera, for this, refer to our Hardware Setup Page

Now we will modify our video settings for your specific needs.

5.2.3. Firewire (DV or IIDC) Video Input

* The current user must be part of the disk and video groups. If that is not the case, then the system administrator must add the "user" to the "disk" group (as shown above)

On Karmic Koala, you have to fix devices attribution for raw1394. Create a udev rule to change group of /dev/raw1394 device: 

echo 'KERNEL=="raw1394", NAME="raw1394", GROUP="disk"' | sudo tee -a /etc/udev/rules.d/99-raw1394.rules
sudo /etc/init.d/udev restart

Furthermore, on all distributions, it is advisable to load the raw1394 module even if no dc1394 cameras are present so that calls to libdc (i.e. milhouse --list-cameras) won't error out. To do this, add raw1394 to the /etc/modules file: 

sudo modprobe raw1394
echo "raw1394" | sudo tee -a /etc/modules

See this bug report for further information.

You can test the DV camera with:

gst-launch dv1394src ! dvdemux ! dvdec ! xvimagesink

5.2.4. Video Test

In Scenic, you can easily preview video4linux2 devices in the Video tab. Other input drivers cannot be used at the moment in Scenic, but milhouse support them:

Choose the videosource according to this table:

v4l2src

video4linux2 devices

v4lsrc

video4linux (v1) devices

dv139src

DV cameras and decks

dc1394src

IIDC 1.31 cameras 

milhouse --localhost --videosource v4l2src

You can add different options such as:

  1. --aspect-ratio

  2. --width

  3. --height

  4. --fullscreen

  5. --flip-video
